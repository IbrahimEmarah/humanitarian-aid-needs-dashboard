# Humanitarian Aid & Refugee Needs Analysis (Data-Driven Crisis Response using Power BI)

## Project Objective
The objective of this project was to provide a "Situation Room" dashboard for NGOs operating in conflict zones. By analyzing the demographic and survival needs of 1,701 displaced families across Syria, Yemen, Iraq, and Libya, the project aimed to ensure that life-saving resources—specifically food and clothing—are distributed to the most vulnerable populations with maximum efficiency.

## Dataset used
- Dataset: <a href="https://github.com/IbrahimEmarah/humanitarian-aid-needs-dashboard/blob/main/Humanitarian%20Needs%20Analysis%20Datasheets.xlsx">Cross-border Humanitarian Assessment Data.</a>
- Scope: 4 Countries (Syria, Yemen, Iraq, Libya) covering 1,701 households and over 4,000 specific aid requirements.

## Questions (KPIs)
- What are the primary survival needs across the four targeted countries?
- How many families are currently in urgent need of Food vs. Clothing?
- What is the gender distribution of the displaced population to ensure sensitive aid allocation?
- Which country (Syria, Yemen, Iraq, or Libya) has the highest concentration of "High-Priority" families?
- How many individuals are represented within the 1,701 families to calculate total bulk resource requirements?
- What is the ratio of children and females within the dataset to plan for specialized medical or nutritional aid?
- Dashboard Interaction <a href="https://github.com/IbrahimEmarah/humanitarian-aid-needs-dashboard/blob/main/Humanitarian%20Needs%20Dashboard.png">View Dashboard</a>

## Process
- Demographic Cleaning: Standardized survey data from various humanitarian field officers to create a unified view of 1,701 families.
- Needs Categorization: Grouped thousands of individual requests into core survival categories, revealing that Food (1,191 requests) and Clothing (704 requests) were the highest priorities.
- Geospatial Mapping: Integrated multi-country data into a single interactive map to visualize the scale of the crisis across the MENA region.
- Dynamic Slicing: Implemented country-specific filters that allow aid workers to drill down from a regional view to a specific country's demographic needs instantly.

![Screenshot of Final Interactive Humanitarian Needs Dashboard](https://github.com/IbrahimEmarah/humanitarian-aid-needs-dashboard/blob/main/Humanitarian%20Needs%20Dashboard.png)

## Project Insight
- Concentrated Crisis: Syria showed the highest concentration of displaced individuals, specifically a high female demographic (2,054 females), necessitating gender-specific aid strategies.
- Primary Survival Gaps: Food and clothing combined make up over 67% of total humanitarian requests, indicating a severe lack of necessities.
- Demographic Sensitivity: The data confirmed a high number of vulnerable individuals (women and children), providing a clear mandate for NGOs to prioritize "Protection and Nutrition" programs.
- Resource Optimization: By identifying that 1,191 families need food, NGOs can move away from "generalized" aid to "targeted" procurement, reducing logistics waste.

## Final Conclusion
To optimize the humanitarian response, aid organizations should prioritize a "Food-First" logistics chain to Syria and Yemen. Additionally, because of the high female-to-male ratio identified in the data, the inclusion of "Dignity Kits" and maternal health resources alongside the 704 clothing requests is essential. This data-driven approach ensures that every dollar of donor funding is directed exactly where the human need is greatest.
